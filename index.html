GREY HARBOR CORRIDOR

OPERATIONAL CONTEXT — READ BEFORE SCENARIO
Area of Operations
Grey Harbor is a contested coastal corridor bordering adversary-controlled territory. The region contains a major logistics port, fuel depot, and key telecommunications infrastructure. Civilian and commercial activity overlaps with potential threat vectors throughout the corridor.
Friendly Mission
Support host nation stability. Protect battalion headquarters and logistics operations. Prevent adversary hybrid escalation from reaching the threshold that would require conventional force response or emergency command action.
Adversary Pattern of Behavior — Escalation Phases
Historically, the adversary escalates in four phases:
  • Phase 1: Information operations and narrative shaping targeting local population
  • Phase 2: Infrastructure disruption — cyber attacks, utility interference
  • Phase 3: Covert staging and false-flag activity to create pretext
  • Phase 4: Conventional force movement under cover of established pretext
Recent Activity (Last 10 Days)
  • Increased coordinated online unrest narratives targeting dockworker communities
  • Minor cyber probing of port scheduling systems — no confirmed disruption yet
  • No confirmed conventional mobilization across northern border
Key Vulnerabilities
  • Larni fuel depot — primary logistics sustainment point
  • Commercial port logistics hub — maritime authority scheduling system
  • Telecommunications switching nodes — emergency comms dependency
  • Water treatment facility — civilian mass-casualty exposure
  • Battalion HQ perimeter — exposed sight lines from residential buildings
Baseline Conditions
Commercial traffic typically operates 0600–1800L. Nighttime military logistics activity is irregular — any confirmed nighttime cargo movement at depots is anomalous. No recent history of conventional staging in this corridor. Checkpoint Delta is the primary northern crossing; staffing gaps documented during weekend rotations.
